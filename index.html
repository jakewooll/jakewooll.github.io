About

I've spent the past decade working on physical products — the kind with supply chains, tolerances, certifications, and retail packaging. That background gives me something most software PMs don't have: an understanding of what it actually takes to ship something into the world, end-to-end.

I'm now moving deliberately into software product management. Not because hardware isn't interesting — it absolutely is — but because the most compelling products I see sit at the intersection of the two. Hardware that's truly useful only because of brilliant software. Devices that become platforms. Physical things that improve after they ship.

I care about clarity: in a brief, in a spec, in a meeting. I find that the discipline of defining a physical product forces a rigour that software teams can sometimes skip. I'm bringing that with me.
